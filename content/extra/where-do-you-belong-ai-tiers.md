# Where Do You Belong?

One of the most common and costly mistakes in AI learning is starting in the wrong place. Many learners either overcomplicate the path or underestimate how quickly they can grow because they do not first define the level they actually need.

## Tier 1 - AI Literacy

This level is for professionals who do not need to build AI systems directly, but need to work effectively with AI in daily business environments.

- Use AI tools confidently
- Communicate AI concepts clearly
- Improve decision quality across functions

## Tier 2 - Applied AI

This level is for people who want to build with AI, not only use it.

- Integrate APIs into products and workflows
- Automate internal processes
- Deploy business tools such as Microsoft Copilot or Azure AI

This tier is often a strong entry point for career switchers and can create meaningful salary upside when applied skills are demonstrated.

## Tier 3 - AI Specialist

This level is for professionals building core AI systems.

- Train and fine-tune models
- Design data and inference pipelines
- Manage infrastructure and operations at scale

This path is highly technical, demanding, and typically aligned with the highest compensation ranges.

## Practical Guidance

- Choose the tier based on your target role, not trends.
- Build proof of capability with projects and outcomes.
- Reassess every 3 to 6 months and move up tiers as needed.
